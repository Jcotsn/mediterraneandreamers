Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-09-03

1. Who we are

Mediterranean Dreamers provides relocation and lifestyle services for retirees on Spain's Mediterranean coast. This policy explains how we handle your personal data in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

2. Contact

For any privacy questions or requests, contact us at hello@mediterraneandreamers.com or on WhatsApp +34 695 099 856.

3. Data we collect

  • Contact form: name, email and message content.
  • Operational communications: if you become a client, we may collect address, phone, identification numbers needed for official procedures, and payment details (as required).
  • Website embeds: our map is provided by Google Maps and may set cookies or collect usage data according to Google’s own policies.

4. Purposes & lawful bases

  • Responding to enquiries (contact form) — consent and/or legitimate interest.
  • Providing contracted services — contract performance.
  • Legal/administrative obligations (e.g., invoicing, tax filings) — legal obligation.
  • Marketing updates (occasional) — consent (you can unsubscribe anytime).

5. Retention

Enquiry data is kept for up to 24 months. Client records are kept as required by law (typically up to 6 years for tax/accounting in Spain) and then securely deleted.

6. Sharing & processors

We share data only when necessary. Typical processors include trusted IT and communication providers. Our contact form is handled by Formspree (form submissions are securely forwarded to our email).

7. International transfers

When data is processed outside the EEA, we rely on adequacy decisions or standard contractual clauses, as applicable.

8. Your rights

You can request access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability and objection to processing. You can also withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.

9. Cookies

We do not use analytics or advertising cookies. Embedded Google Maps may place cookies; you can block third‑party cookies in your browser settings. If we add analytics in the future, we will update this notice and seek consent where required.

10. Complaints

If you believe your rights have been infringed, you may contact the Spanish data protection authority (Agencia Española de Protección de Datos — AEPD) or your local authority. We would appreciate the chance to address your concerns first.

11. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. We will post the new version here and update the “Last updated” date.
